No Shoes Blues - Le Pompé Extraordinaire & The Melodious Vagabonds
A man with his heart set on reaching the end of a nine mile road is burdened with returning to town to buy shoes; the walk so far has taken nine of his toes already. Cobbler Merrygold`s shoe store is the finest in all the land, but with Conman Willy`s manipulative charm, our friend is easily subdued into purchasing shoddy quality shoes. Composition, guitar, recording, mixing and production using my home studio setup.
- C H A M E L E O N -
A 16-minute compositional journey through genre. This was part of my Master of Arts submission portfolio. My Master of Arts explored musical intertextuality in the digital age. Psytrance, House, Reggae, Gypsy Jazz, Folk and Drum & Bass are all entwined to analyse how genres fuse sonically and musically.
Down the Dust Trail ft. Tori Allnutt
Originally an acoustic instrumental piece, I gave the track to my friend Tori to play around with. We ended up crafting the lyrics around a traveller lost in the desert, in desperate search of water. I played the acoustic and bass guitar, mixed and recorded the track. The cymbal swells were recorded during my time at Oxfordshire Music Rooms, the rest was recorded in my home studio.
Lukewarm Rain - Sam Hughes
I produced my friend Sam's track during my placement at Oxfordshire Music Rooms. He is a fantastic folk guitarist and this piece was great fun to record. I love the mixture of Jazz & Folk in this song, one of my classmates from sixth form played the fantastic flute solo and a local Jazz drummer provided the beat. I played bass and slide guitar, mixed, recorded and produced the track.
Miles Away
I had a lot of fun compiling all the videos and layering up the instruments. As you can see I played all the parts on this track, mostly recorded at my home studio, with the drums recorded at Oxfordshire Music Rooms.
Stir it Up - Bob Marley
This cover was recorded in the final year of my Bachelors degree in Phipps Hall, Huddersfield University. This live track was mixed and mastered by myself and recorded by my housemates Sam Broderick and Gareth Savage.
Embryonic Journey - Jefferson Airplane
A cover of the classic Jefferson Airplane song that I played one beautiful afternoon at my parents house in Oxfordshire. This one may be known to a few people as the song that the TV series Friends ended on. I was trying to improve my fingerpicking at the time and this seemed like great way to do it, this one took a lot of practice!
Pickapart - John Butler
A cover of Australian Folk/Rock guitarist John Butler's Pickapart. If you haven't heard of John Butler I thoroughly recommend checking it out. One of the best live performances I have ever seen was him playing with in his trio on a stage that was designed like an old mine shaft in the wild west. With this one I was playing around with a lot of slide guitar, the intro riff is a lot of fun to play!
